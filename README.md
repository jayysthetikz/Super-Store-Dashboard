
## Superstore Sales & Customer Insight Dashboard
A comprehensive Power BI data visualization project analyzing the classic Superstore dataset. This dashboard provides a dual-perspective view: Executive Sales Performance and Granular Customer Analytics, designed to identify growth opportunities and operational efficiencies.

## Key Insights & Features
### Page 1: Sales Performance Overview
Focuses on financial health and market segmentation.

Executive KPIs: Real-time tracking of Net Sales ($3.04K), Net Profit ($401.21), and an Average Discount of 29%.

Segment Analysis: A donut chart highlighting that the Consumer segment drives the majority of revenue (67.2%).

Sales Timeline: An area chart comparing Sales vs. Profit trends across Quarters and Months, identifying peak performance in October.

Regional Performance: Bar charts comparing sales across key states like California, Wisconsin, Florida, and Texas.

Product Deep-Dive: A searchable table with slicers for specific products (e.g., Acco Power Centers) showing detailed order history and quantities.

### Page 2: Customer & Geographic Intelligence
Focuses on individual user behavior and spatial distribution.

Customer Profiling: Dynamic slicers allowing for individual lookups (e.g., Emily Phan) to see their specific contribution.

Geographic Map: A visual distribution of sales across the United States, highlighting high-density regions.

Sales per Category: A detailed breakdown of sales by product sub-categories (Tables, Bookcases, Art, etc.).

Trend Analysis: Tracking the Average Sales and Quantity by Month to understand seasonal buying habits.

## Tech Stack & Tools
Power BI Desktop: For report authoring and UI design.

Power Query: Used for data cleaning and transformation (ETL).

DAX (Data Analysis Expressions): For custom measures like Net Profit and Average Discount.

Fluent UI Design: Styled with a modern blue aesthetic and interactive visual headers.

## Data Source
The analysis is based on the Global Superstore Dataset, which includes detailed information on:

Orders: Product details, sales, profit, and discounts.

Customers: Demographics and geographic locations.

Geography: State and city-level data.

## How to Use
Download the .pbix file from this repository.

Open it using Power BI Desktop.

Interact with the Slicers on Page 1 (Product Name) and Page 2 (Customer ID) to filter the data.

Hover over charts to see detailed tooltips.
